**Spacecraft power system (SPS)** ‚Äî a unit of [spacecraft](sc.md) & [SCS](scs.md) ‚Äî provides [onboard equipment](sc.md) with electricity of the required rating & quality. Usually includes:

   1. **Generators or converters:**
      - [Electric battery](eb.md) (EB):
         1. Capacitor
         1. Galvanic cell
         1. Fueled battery:
            - Biofueled
            - [Nuclear reactor](nr.md) (NR)
            - [Radioisotope thermoelectric generator](rtg.md) (RTG)
            - Other fueled sources
         1. Rechargeable battery (AB)
      - Electro‚Äëmechanical generator:
         1. Flood & flows generator
         1. [Rotor](iu.md)
         1. [Wind turbine](wt.md) (WT)
      - Lightning receiver
      - Mirror system
      - [Solar panel](sp.md)
         1. Optical rectenna
         1. [Solar cell](sp.md)
      - Surface ionized by cosmic rays
      - Thermal‚Äëdifferencies to electricity converter
      - Transmitting from Earth or re‚Äëtransmitter
   1. **Storage device:**
      - Electro‚Äëmechanical accumulators
      - Heat accumulator
      - [Rechargable electric battery](eb.md) (EB)
   1. **Supporting structures:**
      - [Automation & stabilization unit](eas.md) (BAS / KAS)
      - Connectors
      - Electroautomatics
      - Wires

**Sources of an electric energy:**

   - Bioenergy
   - Fuel
   - Geothermal
   - Heat
   - Hydroelectricity
   - Lightnings
   - Marine energy
   - Mechanical
   - Nuclear fuel
   - Solar wind
   - Solar radiation in different spectre
   - Wind

**Other matters:**

   - [Regulated / unregulated voltage](sps.md) (RV / UV)

The voltage produced by each electrochemical cell in a battery is determined by the chemistry of that cell. Cells can be combined in series for multiples of that voltage, or additional circuitry added to adjust the voltage to a different level. Mechanical generators can usually be constructed to any voltage in a range of feasibility.

Nominal voltages of familiar sources:

   - Nerve cell resting potential: ~0.075‚ÄØV
   - Single-cell, rechargeable NiMH or NiCd battery: 1.2‚ÄØV
   - Single-cell, non-rechargeable (e.g., AAA, AA, C & D cells): alkaline battery: 1.5‚ÄØV; zinc-carbon battery: 1.56‚ÄØV if fresh & unused
   - LiFePO‚ÇÑ rechargeable battery: 3.3‚ÄØV
   - Cobalt-based Lithium polymer rechargeable battery: 3.75‚ÄØV (see Comparison of commercial battery types)
   - Transistor-transistor logic/CMOS (TTL) power supply: 5‚ÄØV
   - USB: 5‚ÄØV DC
   - PP3 battery: 9‚ÄØV
   - Automobile battery systems are 2.1 volts per cell; a ‚Äú12V‚Äù battery is 6 cells or 12.6V; a ‚Äú24V‚Äù battery is 12 cells or 25.2‚ÄØV. Some antique vehicles use ‚Äú6V‚Äù 3-cell batteries or 6.3 volts.
   - Electric vehicle battery: 400‚ÄØV when fully charged
   - Household mains electricity AC:
      - 100‚ÄØV in Japan
      - 120‚ÄØV in North America,
      - 230‚ÄØV in Europe, Asia, Africa & Australia
   - Rapid transit third rail: 600‚ÄØ‚Äë‚ÄØ750‚ÄØV (see List of railway electrification systems)
