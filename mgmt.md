## Program & Project manager

A **project** is a short‚Äëtermed entity that comes with dates, budgets, & other resource constraints. Focus on bringing the project‚Äôs need, goals, & objectives on time. Projects have Project Managers. Projects can be combined into a program when the benefits of managing the collection outweigh managing projects as individual units. **Project management** is a tactical position for managing activities of a project & its team: deadlines, budgets, [goals & objectives](rnd.md), resources, time, tasks, etc. A Project manager reports to Program one concerning progress & changes.

A **program** is a large long‚Äëtermed project that is made up of smaller projects which are linked to each other. Focus on a company mission, stakeholder‚Äôs needs. A Program has a Program Manager & a Program Manager‚Äôs Team; projects & tasks delegated across members. **Program management** is a strategic position for planning projects needed to reach major [need & goals](rnd.md): implementation, delegation, goals & objectives definition & articulation, impact for business.

Table. Comparison of management.

|*Basis*|*Project*|*Program*|
|:--|:--|:--|
| Meaning | A project refers to the temporary activity, which is undertaken to create a distinct product or service, that has certain objectives | A program implies a set of projects which are linked to one another, in a sequential manner to attain the combined benefits |
| Focus on | Content | Context |
| Time horizon | Short term | Long term |
| Concerned‚ÄØwith | Specific deliverables, i.e. product or service | Benefits received |
| Functional‚ÄØunits | Single | Multiple |
| Tasks | Technical in nature | Strategic in nature |
| Produces | Output | Outcome |
| Success | Success can be measured in terms of product quality, timeliness, cost effectiveness, compliance & degree of customer satisfaction | Success is measured by the extent to which a program meets out the needs & benefits, for which it was conducted |
