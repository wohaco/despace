##  Mission
The launch took place on June 14, 1967 from Cape Canaveral Air Force Station Launch Complex 12 on Atlas vehicle 5401.

Mariner 5 flew by Venus on October 19 that year at an altitude of 3â€¯990 km ([EVNâ€‘005](venus.md)). With more sensitive instruments than its predecessor [Marinerâ€¯2](mariner_2.md), Mariner 5 was able to shed new light on the hot, cloud-covered planet and on conditions in interplanetary space.

Radio occultation data from Mariner 5 helped to understand the temperature and pressure data returned by the [Veneraâ€¯4](venera_4.md) lander, which arrived at Venus shortly before it. After these missions, it was clear that Venus had a very hot surface and an atmosphere even denser than expected ([EVNâ€‘001](venus.md)).

The operations of Mariner 5 ended in November 1967 and it is now defunct in a heliocentric orbit.
